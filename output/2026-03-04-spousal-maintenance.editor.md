*   **Matrimonial Causes Act 1973:** This Act forms the primary legal framework for financial provision on divorce in England and Wales, including spousal maintenance. It is current as of March 4, 2026.
*   **Practice Direction 27A (Family Proceedings: Court Bundles):** A new version of PD27A is effective from March 2, 2026, mandating electronic bundles (e-bundles) as the default. E-bundles must be searchable PDFs with proper pagination, bookmarking, and a clickable index. The default page limit is 350 A4 pages, only to be exceeded with court permission. It strictly prohibits "unnecessary correspondence" and other specified documents (e.g., general solicitor correspondence, social media messages, full bank statements, photographs) unless specifically directed by the court. Position statements in financial remedy cases now have specific page limits (e.g., 6 pages for First Appointment, 15 for Final Hearing). Non-compliance can lead to sanctions, including cases being removed from the list or adverse costs orders.
*   **Family Procedure Rules (FPR) 3.3 and 3.4 (Non-Court Dispute Resolution):** Significant amendments to FPR Parts 3 and 28, effective April 29, 2024, place a greater emphasis on Non-Court Dispute Resolution (NCDR). Parties must now file Form FM5, detailing their views on NCDR, at least 7 working days before the first hearing. The court can adjourn proceedings for NCDR without consent and may impose cost sanctions on parties who unreasonably refuse to engage in NCDR, as demonstrated in cases like *X v Y EWHC 538 (Fam)*.

**[THE_HURDLE]:**
The primary hurdle for a "traditional" solicitor in spousal maintenance cases in 2026 is navigating the increasingly stringent procedural requirements and the strong judicial push towards Non-Court Dispute Resolution (NCDR). Failure to meticulously prepare electronic court bundles under the new PD27A, adhere to strict page limits, and actively engage in NCDR processes (including filing Form FM5) can lead to significant delays, adverse costs orders, and even cases being removed from the court list, adding complexity and cost for clients.

**[HARD_TRUTHS]:**
1.  **No Automatic Entitlement or Formula:** There is no automatic right to spousal maintenance, nor a fixed formula for its calculation. The court exercises broad discretion based on factors outlined in the Matrimonial Causes Act 1973, focusing on needs, resources, and the marital standard of living.
2.  **Clean Break Principle:** UK courts generally prefer to achieve a "clean break" to sever financial ties between spouses as soon as justly and reasonably possible, often by capitalising maintenance into a lump sum if feasible.
3.  **Termination on Remarriage:** Spousal maintenance orders automatically cease if the recipient remarries or enters a civil partnership, or upon the death of either party.
4.  **Mandatory NCDR Consideration:** Since April 2024, parties are under a heightened obligation to consider and engage in Non-Court Dispute Resolution (NCDR). Unreasonable refusal can lead to adverse costs orders, even if the case proceeds to court.
5.  **Strict Procedural Compliance:** From March 2026, new Practice Direction 27A demands rigorous compliance with electronic court bundle rules, including strict page limits and exclusion of "unnecessary correspondence," with non-compliance risking significant procedural penalties.

Payments typically cease upon the recipient's remarriage or death of either party.
