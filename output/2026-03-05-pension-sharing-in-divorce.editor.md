**[LEGAL_PILLAR]**
*   **Matrimonial Causes Act 1973 (MCA 1973):** This is the core legislation governing divorce and financial provision in England and Wales. Section 25 outlines the factors courts must consider when making financial orders, including pension benefits. Sections 25B to 25D, inserted by the Pensions Act 1995 and amended by the Welfare Reform and Pensions Act 1999, specifically deal with pension attachment and sharing orders.
    *   **Source:** legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1973/18/contents
*   **Welfare Reform and Pensions Act 1999 (WRPA 1999):** This Act introduced the concept of pension sharing into UK law, allowing pension rights to be divided between divorcing spouses as a percentage of their value, creating a clean break.
    *   **Source:** legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1999/30/contents
*   **Family Procedure Rules (FPR) 2010 (as amended April 2024):** These rules govern the practice and procedure in family proceedings. Significant changes effective 29 April 2024 expanded the definition of Non-Court Dispute Resolution (NCDR) and placed a greater expectation on parties to engage in NCDR, with potential cost penalties for unreasonable refusal.
*   **Practice Direction 27A (effective 2 March 2026):** A revised Practice Direction 27A comes into force on 2 March 2026, standardising and modernising rules for court bundles in family proceedings. Key changes include making e-bundles the norm, stricter control over document inclusion (prohibiting "unnecessary correspondence"), and specific page limits for position statements at different hearing stages (e.g., First Appointment: 6 pages, FDR: 12 pages).
    *   **Source:** judiciary.uk/guidance-and-resources/practice-direction-27a-family-proceedings-court-bundles-universal-practice-to-be-applied-in-the-high-court-and-family-court/ (Note: The version currently live on judiciary.uk is dated 31 July 2018 but states it comes into force on 2 March 2026).
