# Everyone in that meeting was bored - even you, the one presenting

I've spent 25 years in the creative arts and performance. For the last nearly 10, I've simultaneously worked inside some of the world's biggest corporations, including BlackRock, Deloitte, JP Morgan, and Google.

The contrast always hits me. In the arts, poor performance has immediate, dire consequences. You won't work again. In business, the same performance gets a calendar invite for next Thursday.

## Why big money keeps getting left on the table

Big money is still being lost to flat pitches and bored rooms. A pitch that fails to land its key message leaves £££ on the table, and nobody gets fired for it. When big money is involved, not investing in how your people communicate is - let's just say - optimistic.

When I work inside investment banks and consultancies, the same pattern shows up again and again: excellent analysis, underpowered delivery.

## Why storytelling remains a buzzword nobody uses

Everyone knows the power of storytelling. Very few actually use it. Why? Most people do not know how, especially when the subject is technical.

The default goes: data gets presented, conclusions get stated, action items get listed. The part that makes a human being actually *care* gets lost on the way. A chart is not a story. A three-point summary is not a story. Story needs stakes, tension, and a human being at the centre.

## Why safe is more expensive than it looks

Same overly cluttered slides. Same tired format. Same "revamped" deck from last year. "That's just how we've always done it" is a phrase I hear in corporate rooms more often than I can explain.

Safe feels cheap because it avoids standing out. In reality it costs far more - in attention lost, deals walked away from, and rooms that tune out the moment the speaker begins. Nobody logs that cost in the budget, but it is real.

## Why voice is the most neglected skill in business

Voicework in business is almost non-existent. Mumbling. Droning. Fading out mid-sentence. Phone-tone monotony in rooms where the cost of a missed message runs into six or seven figures.

In performance training, your voice is your instrument. In business, there is very little awareness of what it costs you not to use it well.

## What the arts and business could actually learn from each other

Don't get me wrong. The arts could learn plenty from business too, specifically: how to make money.

But public speaking, presenting, pitching - like it or not, it's an art form. Let's master the art.
