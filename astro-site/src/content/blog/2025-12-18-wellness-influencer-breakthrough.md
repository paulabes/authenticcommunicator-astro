# How a wellness influencer finally beat her fear of public speaking

Have you ever turned down a speaking opportunity, not because you couldn't do it, but because fear got in the way?

"That photo was of me beaming ear to ear, knowing I'd finally done the thing that had held me back for years. I just can't believe how much of an impact working with you has made. One of the best investments I've ever made."

That message truly made my day. It came from a client who had just smashed through their fear of public speaking.

## The client - a familiar pattern in disguise

This wellness influencer reached out to me over the summer. They had a book, brand deals, and a large social following - but a fear of public speaking that was holding them back.

In their early 20s, they'd suffered a complete mind blank during a presentation. Ever since, public speaking had been a source of deep anxiety. Fast forward to 2025: they're thriving. A published author with brand partnerships, invitations to panels, podcasts, and events, even potential TV opportunities on the horizon.

But the old fear hadn't completely gone away. Every speaking event felt draining. They'd even considered turning down speaking opportunities.

## The full toolkit we worked through together

Together, we worked on tools to help them move through the fear safely. This is the actual list:

- Rehearsing and practising effectively
- Handling tough questions with confidence
- Crafting strong openings and closings
- Acknowledging and processing fears rather than pushing them away
- Moving through avoidance safely
- Practising self-compassion and positive self-talk
- Visualisation techniques
- Using pacing and tone for a calm, confident delivery
- Harnessing body language to look and feel confident
- Creating a pre-performance routine to rely on
- Ongoing personalised guidance between sessions
- Tracking wins with a "box of evidence"

The "box of evidence" is often the piece that clients tell me changes the most. It is a running record of every moment something went well - however small. When fear shows up, the nervous system gets hard evidence to the contrary.

## Why early-career mind-blanks stay lodged in the body

A mind blank in your early 20s does not stay a 20s problem. It lodges in the body as a prediction: *this is what public speaking does to me*.

Every subsequent speaking event gets interpreted through that prediction, which is why success in online or written formats doesn't automatically fix it. The body has to collect a different kind of data - new, lived evidence of speaking going well.

That is what the toolkit above actually delivers. Repeated, specific, good experiences until the prediction starts to shift.
