# Interview coaching success story - from shortlist to offer

A client came to me this month who had been selected for interview at a very exciting and high-profile job.

As a highly qualified person who met all the criteria on paper, she was keen to give herself the best possible chance of success. Meeting the criteria is the entry ticket. Communicating it under pressure is the actual interview.

## The full interview coaching programme we worked through

Here is everything we covered in our sessions. I'm sharing it in full because most people underestimate how much *ground* a properly prepared candidate has to cover.

- Identified her key leadership values and how these tied into both the role and the company she was applying to
- Identified her core skills and how they mapped to the role
- Identified the components and structural additions that make a strong presentation for the scenario-based task
- Prepared a "STAR bank" of answers to potential behaviour and experience-based questions
- Practised pausing when speaking off the cuff
- Practised reversing *upspeak* for more gravitas and authority
- Explored body language for confident off-the-cuff speech
- Prepared and practised answers for common questions
- Ran a full mock interview
- Gave her tools for managing nerves on the day

Each of those is a small skill. Stacked, they are the difference between "met the criteria" and "clearly the person for the job".

## The message that makes this work worth it

A few days after the final interview, I got the message every coach hopes for: she had got the job.

## Why senior candidates invest in interview coaching

This is something I've seen time and time again. Successful people don't hesitate to invest in self-development at pivotal career moments. Communication skills are your greatest asset. Why wouldn't you invest in building them?

## What we can usually cover in a short window before an interview

Even with only two or three sessions before an interview date, a focused programme can cover:

- A STAR-formatted answer bank for the most likely behavioural questions
- A structured approach to the scenario task
- Specific drills for upspeak, filler words, and pacing
- A mock interview with real-time feedback
- A nerves-management protocol for the morning of
