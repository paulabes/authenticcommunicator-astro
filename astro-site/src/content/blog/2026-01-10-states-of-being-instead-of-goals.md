# Goals didn't work. This year, I'm chasing states of being instead.

Did you write down your goals at the start of last year? If so, how did you do?

I don't know about you, but I was thrown so many curveballs and unexpected demands that I didn't reach a single one of mine. I look back with affection at my blindly optimistic January self, blissfully unaware of what 2025 was waiting to throw at me.

I've learned something that might help if, like me, you struggle with goal setting and achieving said goals - no matter how SMART.

## What a "state of being" is (and why it's different to a goal)

A state of being is a quality of experience, not an outcome. Joy, ease, calm, contentment, groundedness, clarity, flow, creativity. My goal this year is to increase the time I spend in these states.

That matters for a few reasons.

Often, when we chase a goal like "earn more money", what we really want is the *state* that money can bring - ease, or a sense of freedom. By bypassing the rigid goal, we aim straight for the jugular, which is the feeling.

## Why states of being hold up better when life is unpredictable

We have very little power over what life events are thrown our way. But we have huge influence over how we feel.

Even when things are genuinely hard, we can still find tiny things that bring us joy. A SMART goal written last January has no say in a disrupted year. A state of being does.

## The practice - mapping states to small, repeatable choices

Once you've named the states you want to spend more time in, the next step is listing the small ways to unlock them.

For example, a key to joy might be singing, time with my children, being in nature, or feeling sunlight on my face. A key to clarity might be a ten-minute walk without a phone. A key to ease might be saying no to the third Friday call.

Ask yourself:

- What are your keys to joy, ease, or clarity?
- What other states would you like to spend more time in?
- How could you practise them more consciously in a normal week?

## What this has to do with public speaking

When it comes to public speaking, the same question applies. How do you want to feel when you speak? What states do you want your voice to convey? Authority, warmth, steadiness, curiosity?

Build the practices that unlock those states. A short breath pattern before you walk on. A slower pace for the first minute. A specific memory that puts warmth in your voice.
