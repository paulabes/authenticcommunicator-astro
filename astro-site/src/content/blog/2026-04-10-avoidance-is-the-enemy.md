# Avoidance, not fear, is the real enemy of public speaking

You think fear of public speaking is your biggest enemy? It isn't. Fear isn't the enemy. Avoidance is.

Avoidance looks like saying "no" to a speaking opportunity. Avoiding practice by never *finding time*. Refusing to record yourself. Telling yourself you're terrible and even trying is pointless.

## Why avoidance feels safe but makes fear worse

Avoidance feels safe. Avoidance feels easy. But every time you avoid, you feed the fear - and the more you feed fear, the more it grows.

Avoidance robs you of the chance to build evidence that you can do it. You will never know what it feels like to speak well because you never gave yourself the chance to try.

Here's the hard truth: no one can break the avoidance cycle but you. If you stay in avoidance, you will not improve.

## A story about breaking the cycle in real time

I saw the power of overcoming avoidance in a recent group workshop.

One delegate was visibly nervous. Her breathing was shallow, her shoulders gripped at her ears. The group encouraged her, and eventually she gained the courage to speak. And she was amazing. Funny. Engaging. Someone who can genuinely hold a room.

Afterwards, I asked if she'd go again. Her face dropped. Avoidance kicked in immediately:

- "I've done it once, that's enough."
- "What if it's worse this time?"

It hit me again how clever our brains are at finding ways to sidestep discomfort. But avoidance doesn't lead to growth - it keeps us stuck.

So, with gentle encouragement, I asked her to go again. And she smashed it. Again.

## What repeated exposure actually builds

Repeated exposure builds three things that fear alone can't touch:

- The knowledge, and more importantly the evidence, that you can rise to the challenge
- The evidence that you can do it *repeatedly*, not just once as a lucky one-off
- The bodily memory and imprint of what it feels like when it goes well

That's what builds real, durable confidence. Not affirmations. Not perfect conditions. Reps.

## What to do if you are stuck in the cycle

If you struggle with fear around public speaking (like, what, 77% of the population?), here's my most heartfelt wish for you.

Push through your avoidance. Once. Twice. A hundred times. Not expecting perfection, just expecting progress.

Because every time you show up, you collect another piece of proof:

- You are capable
- You are growing
- You *are* a confident public speaker in the making
